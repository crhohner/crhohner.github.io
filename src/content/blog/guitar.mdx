In the spring and summer of 2022, I built an acoustic guitar through the <a href = "https://www.specimenproducts.com/chicago-school-of-guitar-making/" >Chicago School of Guitar Making. </a>
I had stumbled into the studio a year before on an assignment from my high school newspaper, <a href = "https://uhighmidway.com/staff_name/caroline-hohner/">the U-High Midway.</a>

The atelier was filled to the brim with serious manufacturing equipment, beautiful instruments, and highly specialized tools.
It was also full of oddities like a guitar with a pickguard made of sardines, jars of dried figs and beetle carapaces,
and the dismembered arm of a baby doll.

My bizarre and captivating visit stuck with me until a year later when I began to plan my senior capstone project.

That winter, I reached back out to the studio's owner and asked to join his upcoming acoustic guitar class that May. That spring, I quickly found out that
this project would not be something I could knock out in just one month. Between the amount of time spent sanding, waiting for glue to dry,
sanding again, and waiting for the humidity to die down, there was a lot of what seemed like dead time during the project.

Luckily, the studio wasn't too far from home, and I was able to spend nearly every morning from May to August in the studio. Throughout the project,
I learned to use fun tools like chisels and jewelry saws. I also learned to use tools that terrified me, like bare razor blades and bandsaws.
Beyond any new technical skills I picked up, I developed an appreciation for the unrelenting precision and confidence needed to produce a quality craft.
